# Hallmark Before Audit - Homepage Full Rebuild

Target: `app/page.tsx`, `app/globals.css`

## Context

Hallmark is available at `.agents/skills/hallmark/SKILL.md` and was read before implementation. Hermes is explicitly out of scope for this run.

Audience: one-person household users, younger family/referrers, and people who may receive SMS as contacts.
Use case: understand the product quickly and decide whether to open the App Store or review the referrer guide.
Tone: Quiet Luxury Trust; premium public-service editorial, not generic SaaS or fashion luxury.

## Findings

### Major

- The current page is correct but visually under-authored. It uses an orderly split hero, rule panel, rails, and simple timelines, but the rhythm still reads like a clean CSS exercise rather than a finished brand page.
- The product visual is too close to a dashboard/table. It shows real facts, but the composition is a bordered panel with rows, so it does not feel like a crafted product artifact.
- The 48h rule is not yet the signature visual. The current line-and-dot timeline is legible, but it is too familiar and does not become a memorable page asset.
- The price block is honest but visually loud. The large amount risks feeling promotional instead of contract-like and premium.
- Lower links and fact rails are improved from plain tables, but their structure still relies heavily on borders and rows.

### Minor

- The background grid still hints at design-tool wireframe language.
- Section spacing is consistent but not editorial enough; the page lacks deliberate asymmetry.

## Required Redesign Move

Adopt one direction: Quiet Luxury Trust as a premium editorial product sheet.

Discarded direction: generic luxury treatment with dark surfaces, gold accents, serif display type, marble texture, or oversized brand theatrics. It would conflict with readability, safety, and the product's modest monthly price.

## Guardrails

- Preserve confirmed copy, price, consent rule, limitation copy, and CTA IDs.
- No new marketing claim, no external assets, no external fonts, no dependency change.
- No fake phone, no admin dashboard, no medical/security-company visual language.
